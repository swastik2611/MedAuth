Counterfeit and substandard medicines pose a serious threat to public health and safety, particularly in developing countries where regulatory frameworks are weaker. Such drugs not only lack the intended therapeutic effect but can also cause severe harm to patients. In recent years, several technological solutions have been proposed to tackle this issue, including blockchain-based systems. Blockchain technology provides an immutable and decentralized ledger that can be used to track the movement of goods and services, making it an ideal solution for supply chain management. In this paper, we propose a blockchain-based medicine verification system that utilizes QR code technology to verify the authenticity and provenance of medicines. This system has the potential to increase trust and transparency in the pharmaceutical industry, reduce the prevalence of counterfeit medicines, and improve patient safety.
